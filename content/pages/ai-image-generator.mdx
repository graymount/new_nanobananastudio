## Why Most AI Generators Fail at Text in Images

**1. Token-to-pixel gap.** The language model understands words. The image model generates pixels. Meaning and spelling get lost between the two.

**2. No character-level precision.** Diffusion models optimize for overall image quality, not individual letter accuracy. A shift of 2-3 pixels turns an "r" into an "n."

**3. Random noise starting point.** Every generation begins from different random noise, so the same prompt produces different spelling errors each time.

These are architectural limitations, not prompt problems. For the full technical explanation, read [why AI image generators struggle with text in images](/blog/why-ai-struggles-with-text-in-images).

Diffusion models learn pixel patterns, not letter sequences. They generate shapes that statistically resemble text but don't understand spelling. This gap between language understanding and pixel generation is why AI text rendering remains unreliable.

Short text (1-5 words) is most reliable. Keep text elements short and generate one text block per image for best results.
