## The Core Principle: Descriptive Storytelling

The most effective approach to image generation is **descriptive storytelling**. Rather than listing disconnected keywords, Nano Banana's AI excels when given "a narrative, descriptive paragraph" that contextualizes the scene.

**Instead of this:**
```
dog, park, sunny, happy, 4k, masterpiece
```

**Write this:**
```
A golden retriever playing fetch in a sun-dappled park on a warm
autumn afternoon. The dog is mid-leap, catching a red frisbee,
with fallen leaves swirling around. Warm golden hour lighting.
```

## The Prompt Framework

Structure your prompts using this framework for consistent results:

```
[Subject + Adjectives] doing [Action] in [Location].
[Composition]. [Lighting]. [Style].
```

**Example:**
> A stoic robot barista with glowing blue optics serving coffee in a futuristic cafe. Wide shot. Warm morning light. Cinematic style.

## 6 Proven Prompt Strategies

### 1. Photorealistic Scenes

Include photography terminology for realistic images:
- Camera angles (low angle, bird's eye view)
- Lens types (85mm portrait lens, wide-angle)
- Lighting conditions (golden hour, studio lighting)
- Specific textures and materials

**Example:**
> Portrait of a jazz musician in a dimly lit club. Captured with an 85mm lens, shallow depth of field with soft bokeh. Dramatic spotlight from above, smoke haze in the air.

### 2. Stylized Illustrations

Be explicit about artistic style and visual characteristics:

**Example:**
> A cute fox character in Studio Ghibli style, soft watercolors, whimsical forest background. Gentle morning light filtering through the trees.

### 3. Accurate Text Rendering

For logos, posters, or infographics, describe fonts descriptively and include exact text:

**Example:**
> A vintage concert poster with the text "JAZZ NIGHT" in bold red serif font at the top center. Art deco style geometric patterns, black and gold color scheme.

### 4. Product Photography

Specify studio setups for commercial-quality images:

**Example:**
> Professional product photo of a luxury watch on a dark marble surface. Three-point studio lighting, soft reflections, 45-degree angle. Clean white background with subtle shadows.

### 5. Minimalist Compositions

Emphasize negative space for designs meant as backdrops:

**Example:**
> Minimalist landscape with vast empty sky taking up two-thirds of the frame. Single small tree silhouette on the horizon. Soft gradient from pale blue to warm peach.

### 6. Sequential Art

For consistent multi-panel storytelling:

**Example:**
> Comic book panel showing a superhero landing on a rooftop at night. Bold outlines, vibrant colors, dramatic perspective. Include motion lines and a speech bubble saying "I'm here."

## Lighting: The Mood Setter

Lighting dramatically affects the emotional tone of your images:

| Lighting Type | Mood/Effect |
|--------------|-------------|
| Golden hour | Warm, nostalgic, romantic |
| Blue hour | Mysterious, melancholic |
| Harsh midday | Dramatic, intense |
| Candlelight | Intimate, cozy |
| Neon lights | Cyberpunk, futuristic |
| Studio softbox | Clean, professional |
| Backlit/silhouette | Dramatic, ethereal |

**Always specify time of day** to anchor your lighting:
> Portrait of a woman at a cafe. **Late afternoon golden light** streaming through the window, casting long shadows.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Keyword Spam
**Don't:** "beautiful, stunning, 4k, masterpiece, best quality, highly detailed"
**Do:** Describe what makes it beautiful with specific details.

### 2. Negative Framing
**Don't:** "A street with no cars, no people, no trash"
**Do:** "An empty, pristine cobblestone street at dawn"

### 3. Vague Descriptions
**Don't:** "A fantasy character"
**Do:** "An elven archer in ornate silver plate armor etched with leaf patterns, wielding a recurve bow"

### 4. Ignoring Composition
**Don't:** Just describe subjects
**Do:** Include framing: "Close-up portrait," "Wide establishing shot," "Over-the-shoulder view"

## Iterative Refinement

Google's documentation emphasizes: **"Don't expect a perfect image on your first attempt."**

Use multi-turn conversation to refine:

1. **Initial prompt:** "A cozy reading nook by a window"
2. **Refinement:** "Add more plants and warmer lighting"
3. **Detail:** "Make the window show a rainy day outside"
4. **Final touch:** "Add a sleeping cat on the chair"

This iterative approach often yields better results than trying to specify everything upfront.
